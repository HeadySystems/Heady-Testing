# International Filing Timeline
## Heady™ Latent OS — PCT / EPO / WIPO Strategy
### Version 1.0 | March 2026

> **DISCLAIMER**: This document is for internal planning purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. International patent filing is complex and jurisdiction-specific. All deadlines and filing decisions must be reviewed by qualified patent counsel with international filing experience. Missing international filing deadlines results in permanent loss of rights in those jurisdictions.

---

## Executive Summary

Heady's 60+ provisional patents cover inventions that are commercially valuable in multiple jurisdictions. The PCT (Patent Cooperation Treaty) route provides a cost-effective method to preserve international patent rights for up to 30–31 months from the provisional priority date before committing to country-specific prosecution costs.

**Recommended Core Strategy**:
1. File U.S. non-provisional + PCT application simultaneously at Month 11 (before the 12-month provisional deadline)
2. Use the PCT international search report (received ~Month 16–18) to evaluate claim strength before committing national phase costs
3. Enter national phase in strategic jurisdictions at Month 28–30

## Part 4: Strategic Country Selection for AI Patents

### Priority Tier 1 — File in All Cases

| Jurisdiction | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **United States** | Largest AI commercial market; strongest enforcement; home jurisdiction |
| **European Patent Office (EPO)** | Single filing covers 39 countries; EU AI regulation creates licensing leverage; strong enforcement in Germany, UK, France, Netherlands |

### Priority Tier 2 — File Based on Business Traction

| Jurisdiction | Rationale | AI Patent Climate |
|---|---|---|
| **China (CNIPA)** | World's largest AI patent filer; massive domestic market; essential for licensing deals with Chinese tech companies | Increasingly favorable for software/AI if applied aspects are emphasized; avoid "algorithm-only" claims |
| **Japan (JPO)** | Strong IP enforcement; major AI application market; potential licensing partners (Sony, Toyota, NTT) | Generally favorable for AI; JPO has been more liberal than EPO on software; technical character requirement applies |
| **South Korea (KIPO)** | Samsung, LG, Hyundai are major AI investors; strong semiconductor/edge AI market | Favorable; KIPO fast prosecution (~18 months) and lower cost ISA option |

### Priority Tier 3 — File if Resources Available

| Jurisdiction | Rationale | AI Patent Climate |
|---|---|---|
| **Canada** | Adjacent U.S. market; similar legal framework; potential Canadian tech company licensees | Generally permissive; follow U.S. Alice guidance loosely |
| **United Kingdom** | Post-Brexit separate from EPO; strong fintech AI market; UK Supreme Court 2026 "intermediate step" framework | Stricter than U.S. on software; technical contribution must be demonstrated |
| **India** | 1.4B person market; growing AI startup ecosystem; Section 3(k) challenges require technical framing | Section 3(k) prohibits patent on "mathematical or business method or a computer programme per se" — requires demonstrating technical application |
| **Australia** | English-speaking; strong patent enforcement; growing tech sector | Generally favorable; follows UK/EPO approach with some differences |
| **Singapore** | IP-friendly jurisdiction; ASEAN gateway; strong enforcement | Very favorable for AI/software; modeled on UK approach |

### AI-Specific Jurisdictional Guidance

**EPO Special Considerations** (high-value but strict):
- EPO rejected 69% of software/AI patent claims in 2025 (per J A Kemp analysis)
- Strategy: Every claim must state a **technical effect** beyond the computation itself
- Edge AI inference (Patent #6) → strongest EPO candidate (hardware resource management)
- 3D Vector Memory (Patent #3) → strong EPO candidate (memory management improvement)
- CSL Gates (Patent #1) → must emphasize specific hardware execution and measurable outcomes
- Sacred Geometry topology (Patent #2) → must tie to network bandwidth/routing efficiency

**China CNIPA Special Considerations**:
- Since 2017 AI patent guidelines, China allows software patents with "technical means to solve technical problems"
- Functional claims covering specific technical implementations are generally allowed
- Avoid claims reading only on pure algorithms or mathematical concepts
- Consider filing invention patents + utility models (shorter examination, 10-year term) for faster protection

**India Section 3(k) Strategy**:
- Claims must demonstrate a **technical application** beyond mathematical computation
- Frame claims around hardware implementations and specific technical outcomes
- Include claims tied to network infrastructure, memory hardware, or processor improvements

## Part 6: PCT National Phase — Country-by-Country Entry Checklist

### For Each Target Jurisdiction, Complete at Month 28:

**United States National Phase**:
- [ ] File via USPTO Patent Center as a national stage application (371 application)
- [ ] Pay basic national fee, search fee, examination fee
- [ ] File IDS citing PCT ISR references
- [ ] Confirm small/micro entity status for fee purposes
- [ ] Consider filing request for examination (without separate RCE needed)

**EPO (European Patent)**:
- [ ] File via EPO Online Filing system
- [ ] Pay: filing fee (~€1,460), search fee (~€1,900), designation fee, extension fees
- [ ] File claims in English, French, or German (English preferred)
- [ ] EPO will send Supplemental European Search Report (SESR) if ISR was from non-EPO ISA
- [ ] Respond to SESR/ESOP (equivalent to US non-final OA) within 6 months
- [ ] Validation: upon grant, validate in each desired EPC member state individually
  - Germany, UK (pre-separation), France, Netherlands, Switzerland are highest priority

**China (CNIPA)**:
- [ ] Engage Chinese local agent (required — foreign applicants cannot file directly)
- [ ] Provide Chinese translation of specification and claims
- [ ] File at CNIPA within 30 months of priority date
- [ ] Request examination (substantive examination is not automatic — must be requested within 3 years of filing)
- [ ] Budget 2–4 years for examination + 1–2 Office Actions

**Japan (JPO)**:
- [ ] Engage Japanese local agent (required)
- [ ] Provide Japanese translation of specification and claims
- [ ] File at JPO within 30 months of priority date
- [ ] Request examination within 3 years of filing date (otherwise deemed abandoned)

## Part 7: CIP and Continuation Strategy for International Portfolio

### Rolling CIP Architecture for Heady™

Per the research in section6_patent_strategy.md, an ongoing CIP strategy keeps patent families alive for iterative AI platforms:

```
Year 0-1:  File provisionals → Convert to non-provisional + PCT
           ↓
Year 1-2:  Enter national phase (US, EPO, CN, JP)
           ↓
Year 2-3:  File CIP1 from any U.S. patent while parent still pending
           (must be within 6 years of EBD to avoid $2,700 surcharge)
           → Adds new vector memory subsystem features
           ↓
Year 3-4:  File CIP2 from CIP1 while CIP1 still pending
           → Adds new edge AI inference features
           ↓
Year 4-5:  File CIP3 from CIP2 while CIP2 still pending
           → Adds MCP meta-server protocol extensions
```

**Key rules for international CIP strategy**:
- A PCT application can claim priority to a U.S. CIP application
- However: the PCT will only receive the **CIP's priority date** for new matter
- Old matter in CIP retains the parent's priority date
- File PCT within 12 months of CIP filing to preserve international rights for new matter

### Avoiding the 6-Year Surcharge

As of January 19, 2025, USPTO charges surcharges for continuing applications filed more than 6 years after EBD:
- 6–9 years after EBD: +$2,700 (small entity: +$1,080)
- 9+ years after EBD: +$4,000 (small entity: +$1,600)

**Action**: Schedule CIP filings proactively. Create calendar reminders at the 5.5-year EBD mark for each provisional to evaluate CIP opportunities.
