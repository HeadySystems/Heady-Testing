# Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how the applicable Heady entity identified on the relevant website, application, or service collects, uses, shares, and retains personal information.

## Scope
This policy applies to Heady websites, applications, APIs, developer tools, support channels, and related services that link to this notice.

## Information collected
We may collect account information, contact details, authentication identifiers, billing data, support communications, device and usage information, diagnostics, and content submitted through service features.

## How information is used
We use information to provide and secure the services, authenticate users, process payments, operate support, improve product performance, prevent abuse, investigate incidents, and comply with legal obligations.

## Legal bases
Where privacy laws such as GDPR apply, we may rely on contract necessity, legitimate interests, consent where required, legal obligations, and other lawful bases appropriate to the processing context.

## Sharing
We may disclose information to infrastructure, hosting, analytics, communications, support, error-monitoring, payment, and AI service providers acting on our behalf under contractual restrictions, and where required by law or corporate transaction.

## Retention
We retain personal information only as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including security, accounting, compliance, dispute-resolution, and legal-hold needs. See the internal retention schedule for operational detail.

## Rights
Depending on location, individuals may have rights to access, delete, correct, port, restrict, object, or opt out of certain processing. California and Colorado residents may have additional rights concerning sharing, sensitive data, and opt-out signals.

## International transfers
Where data moves across borders, we use legally required safeguards as appropriate.

## Security
We maintain administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect personal information, but no method of storage or transmission is completely secure.
