### Scenario Narrative

**Conservative:** Community growth is slower than expected due to competitive landscape. Cursor and GitHub Copilot dominate developer mindshare. Enterprise sales cycles extend to 4+ months. SOC 2 Type II delay pushes some enterprise deals to Year 2. Revenue still reaches $700K ARR by end of Year 1 — sufficient for seed funding on favorable terms.

**Base:** Expected trajectory. Open-source community grows steadily via GitHub, HN, and conference talks. SOC 2 Type I in Month 4 unlocks enterprise pipeline. 1–2 enterprise logos close in Month 5–6 generating credibility for subsequent deals. 32% monthly MRR growth from Month 3–12. Reaches $2.2M ARR at Year 1 exit — strong Series A positioning.

**Optimistic:** Viral open-source moment (Product Hunt, HN front page) in Month 3–4 drives rapid community adoption. Enterprise buyers — particularly EU companies facing AI Act compliance pressure — accelerate deal velocity. 2–3 anchor enterprise logos ($15K+/mo ACV) drive NRR above 120%. Reaches near $5M ARR at Year 1 exit — exceptional by any benchmark.

### LTV / CAC Analysis (Base Case)

**Developer Plan:**
- ARPU: $29/mo
- Monthly churn: 4.5% → Avg. customer lifetime: 22.2 months
- LTV: $29 × 22.2 = **$644**
- Estimated CAC (PLG, low-touch): $35 (content/DevRel attribution)
- **LTV:CAC = 18:1** (well above 3:1 healthy minimum; PLG flywheel at work)

**Team Plan:**
- ARPU: $792/mo (8 seats × $99)
- Monthly churn: 1.5% → Avg. lifetime: 66.7 months
- LTV: $792 × 66.7 = **$52,826**
- CAC: $1,200 (account executive time, demo calls)
- **LTV:CAC = 44:1** (exceptional — land-and-expand from Developer tier)

**Enterprise:**
- ARPU: $9,000/mo (NRR 112% → growing)
- Monthly churn: 0.5% → Avg. lifetime: 200 months
- LTV: $9,000 × 200 × 1.12 NRR = **$2,016,000** (NRR-adjusted)
- CAC: $18,000 (AE time, security reviews, POC)
- **LTV:CAC = 112:1** (sovereign AI structural lock-in driving extraordinary retention)

## 7. Comparison to Cursor / Windsurf / Supabase

### Trajectory Benchmarks at Key Milestones

| Company | $1M ARR | $10M ARR | $100M ARR | Key Growth Driver |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cursor** | ~Month 6 | ~Month 12 | ~Month 18 | PLG + VS Code ecosystem integration |
| **Windsurf** | ~Month 8 | ~Month 18 | N/A (acquired) | Credit-based freemium, generous free tier |
| **Supabase** | ~Month 12 | ~Month 20 | ~Month 36 | Open-source + AI app wave (Bolt.new) |
| **Replit** | ~Month 18 | ~Month 30 | ~Month 45 | Consumer/vibe-coding pivot |
| **Heady (Base)** | ~Month 14–16 | ~Month 24–28 | ~Month 42–48 | PLG → sovereign enterprise expansion |
| **Heady (Optimistic)** | ~Month 10–12 | ~Month 18–22 | ~Month 36–40 | EU AI Act compliance wave + defense |

### Key Differences in Heady's Model vs. Cursor

| Dimension | Cursor | Heady |
|---|---|---|
| Primary market | Professional developers (code) | Sovereign AI infrastructure (all enterprise) |
| Free-to-paid driver | VS Code habit + completion quality | BYOM value + agent swarm capability |
| Enterprise average ACV | ~$24K (40 seats × $40/yr) | ~$72K (10+ seats × $99/mo or custom) |
| Sovereignty features | Limited (cloud-first) | Core differentiator (VPC, air-gap, HIPAA) |
| NRR target | ~110% | ~115–125% (sovereign lock-in) |
| CAC strategy | Pure PLG (no sales team early) | PLG + inbound enterprise from Year 1 |
| Regulatory tailwind | None | EU AI Act, US CLOUD Act, HIPAA, FedRAMP |

### Why Heady Has a Credible Path to Cursor-Like Scale

1. **Larger per-seat price:** $99/seat/mo Team vs. $40/seat/mo Cursor Business. At equal seat counts, Heady generates 2.5× more ARR.
2. **Enterprise ACV premium:** Sovereign/compliant AI commands 3–5× premium per Tabnine's pricing data ([Tabnine Pricing](https://www.tabnine.com/pricing/)).
3. **Regulatory forcing function:** EU AI Act (August 2026) and HIPAA mandates create procurement urgency that Cursor doesn't benefit from.
4. **Structural lock-in:** On-premises deployments have near-zero switching costs (no need to re-certify security, re-migrate vectors). This drives enterprise NRR above 120%.
5. **Dual-entity model:** HeadyConnection nonprofit maintains community trust while HeadySystems monetizes enterprise — reduces the "corporate takeover" risk that alienated MongoDB/Elastic communities.

### Comparable Revenue Milestones Context

- **Supabase** took ~18 months to reach $1M ARR from open-source launch, then accelerated to $30M ARR as Bolt.new wave created passive distribution. Heady has an analogous opportunity via sovereign AI regulations as the "forcing wave."
- **Cursor** reached $100M ARR in ~18 months from launch — but required $500M+ in funding and pre-existing VS Code ecosystem. Heady's path is longer but requires significantly less capital due to the enterprise-first model.
- **Windsurf** at $100M ARR (April 2025) was acquired for ~$3B — a 75× revenue multiple reflecting AI infrastructure scarcity value. Sovereign AI infrastructure likely commands similar multiples given the regulatory moats.

## 8. Sensitivity Analysis

### Revenue Sensitivity to Key Variables (Base Year 1 = $744,992)

| Variable | -50% | -25% | Base | +25% | +50% |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free sign-up growth rate | $312K | $528K | $745K | $960K | $1.18M |

**Second most impactful:** Enterprise contracts closed ($465K–$1.02M range). Each enterprise deal adds $6,000–$12,000 MRR and compounds via NRR. One additional enterprise deal per quarter generates $200K+ incremental Year 1 revenue.
