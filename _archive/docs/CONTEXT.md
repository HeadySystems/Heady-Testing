## 3. Trust Zones & Information Flow

All data and execution must be classified into one of five Trust Zones. The Heady™Conductor is responsible for enforcing zone transitions.

- **Zone A (Safe Text):** UI/Client interactions. Unverified intent. Safe for public display.
- **Zone B (Internal Read):** Backend API. Authenticated but constrained. No decryption of sensitive secrets.
- **Zone C (Sensitive Read / Constrained Write):** Workspace Engine. Access to decrypted context and proprietary data.
- **Zone D (Regulated Write):** High-impact digital actions (Email, Database Writes). Requires Approval Service quorum.
- **Zone E (Actuation):** Physical impact (Robotics, Smart Locks). Requires Simulation Sandbox success + Physical Presence (PTACA).

**Flow Mandate:**

- All writes are deny-by-default.
- Zone D/E operations require an ActionRequest (RAA) with a valid Capability Token.
- Audit logs must be append-only and hash-chained.
